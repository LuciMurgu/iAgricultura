# Farmer Discovery Summary

## Background

This summary reflects the foundational customer discovery that drove the shift in AgroUnu's architecture from isolated technical ledgers (modules) to Outcome-Based Navigation.

*(Note: Based on early discovery documentation and pilot structuring available in the Farm Operating Picture framework. Exact quotes from pending live pilot notes will be added here as they become available.)*

## What Farmers Said They Need

Farmers are overwhelmed by fragmented software. They do not want another "dashboard" or a complex technical tool to learn. Their operational stress revolves around distinct, high-impact business outcomes.

### 1. Funding
Accessing credit, leasing equipment, and applying for grants (APIA, AFIR) is bureaucratic and slow. Farmers need their data organized specifically to prove they are bankable and eligible.
- **Product Implication:** The system must evaluate "Funding Readiness" based on the completeness of the Farm Context Pack and Evidence Vault.

### 2. Buy Cheaper
Input costs (fertilizer, seeds, fuel) are volatile and represent the largest upfront risk. Farmers suspect they are overpaying compared to regional averages but lack the data to prove it.
- **Product Implication:** A Procurement Review system that analyzes invoices to surface anomalies and compare prices against cooperative/regional signals.

### 3. Sell Better
Harvest timing and market access dictate profitability. Farmers often sell at harvest when prices are lowest due to lack of storage or cash-flow pressure.
- **Product Implication:** Storage & Sale Readiness tracking, combined with Scenario Sandboxing (e.g., "What if I store for 3 months?").

### 4. Practical Knowledge
General advice is useless. Farmers need agronomic and compliance knowledge that is directly mapped to their specific county, soil type, and crop rotation.
- **Product Implication:** A Trusted Knowledge Library queried only through the lens of the specific Farm Context.

### 5. Documents
Finding the right lease agreement, invoice, or quality certificate when an inspector or accountant asks is a massive time sink.
- **Product Implication:** A centralized Evidence Vault linked directly to the FOP ledgers.

### 6. Trust and Cooperation
Farmers are inherently skeptical of sharing data unless there is a clear, mutual benefit. They are interested in cooperative pooling but fear losing negotiating leverage.
- **Product Implication:** Strict Trust/Sharing controls and anonymous Regional Intelligence aggregation.

### 7. Less Risk
The ultimate goal is peace of mind. Farmers want to know they aren't missing critical deadlines or bleeding cash.
- **Product Implication:** Proactive "Deciziile săptămânii" (Weekly Decisions) surfacing on the dashboard, driven by the FOP.

## Conclusion

The discovery clearly indicated that a "feature-heavy" app would fail. AgroUnu must guide the farmer toward these specific outcomes while aggressively managing the data gaps (Missing Data model) that prevent those outcomes.
